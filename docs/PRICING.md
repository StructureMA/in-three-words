# In Three Words — Pricing & Financials

## Tier Structure

Two tiers at launch. The 37+ inch tier is shelved until volume justifies the shipping cost.

| Size | Price | Charity Donation | You Keep | Est. Shipping | Margin |
|------|-------|-----------------|----------|---------------|--------|
| Under 11x14 | $20 | $5 | $15 | $10–12 | $3–5 |
| 12x16 – 24x36 | $25 | $7 | $18 | $15–20 | $0–3 |

## Entry & Payment Model

- **Entry is free** — no deposit, no upfront payment
- Entrants submit: name, phone number, 3 words, preferred size
- One person is selected at random each Sunday
- Selected person is **texted** and has **3 hours to confirm and pay**
- If they don't respond, the next name is drawn
- Payment via Venmo or PayPal

## Why This Model

- **Zero transaction fees on entries** — no charges, no refunds, no processing overhead
- **Legally clean** — no money changes hands until after selection, so it's not a lottery
- **Builds a contact list** — phone numbers are collected with each entry
- **Creates urgency** — the 3-hour window keeps things moving
- **Filters for commitment** — if someone doesn't pay, you just pick the next person

## Key Decisions

- **Flat charity donation** (not a percentage split) — keeps the charity story intact while ensuring solvency
- **US shipping only** at launch — international shipping is cost-prohibitive at this scale
- **Pirate Ship** for discounted USPS rates — significantly cheaper than going direct

## Break-Even Estimate (4 paintings/month)

- Revenue: ~$85–100/month
- Charity donations: $20–28/month
- Shipping: ~$40–60/month
- Remaining for supplies: ~$15–30/month

The goal at launch isn't profit — it's building a name. Margins improve as volume and audience grow.

## Future Pricing Levers

- Bulk supply purchases lower cost per piece
- Better shipping rates with volume
- Price increases as demand grows
- Print reproductions as passive revenue
- Corporate/gift commissions at higher price points
